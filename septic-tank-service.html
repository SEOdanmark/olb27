What is the role of bacteria in maintaining a healthy septic system?

These microscopic organisms are responsible for decomposing solids, fats, oils, and grease in the septic tank, allowing for proper drainage of liquid effluent into the drain field. In a septic system, bacteria work tirelessly to break down waste materials through a process called biodegradation.

Without these helpful bacteria, solid waste would accumulate in the tank, leading to clogs and backups that could potentially damage the entire system. There are two main types of bacteria that are essential for maintaining a healthy septic system: aerobic and anaerobic bacteria.
